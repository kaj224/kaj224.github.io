My research sits at the intersection of public finance, labor economics, and urban economics. I study how racial and demographic change shapes public goods provision, local policy decisions, and labor-market outcomes.

Works in Progress

Black Flight and Local Public Goods
Examines how Black suburbanization influences local public goods provision and institutional responses in suburban municipalities.

The Impact of Manufacturing Decline on the New Great Migration
Studies how deindustrialization in Northern metros has shaped Black migration patterns and economic outcomes in Southern labor markets.
